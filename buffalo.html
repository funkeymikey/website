Food Trucks

There is a food truck movement happening in the city. It seems like a new one is popping up every month. They show up at various events around the community and make really good food.

To stay up to date, you can visit wutsupbuffalo.com to see all the food trucks and see where they are scheduled to show up.

Local and organic options

As a coworker once said, if Buffalo is ever overrun by zombies, there'd be a standoff at the co-op fighting over free range eggs.

Lexington co-op is our local cooperative market in the Elmwood Village neighborhood. It's grown so much that they're opening up a second location in another neighborhood off of North Buffalo on Hertel Avenue.

Farmer's markets exist all over the region. The most popular is on Saturday morning in Elmwood Village. But there's so many other ones in North Buffalo, Downtown, or near the University every week - and that's just in the city!

CSAs are popular in the area. With a multitude to choose from, you can get local and organic vegetables every week. There's also the standard fruit shares, dairy shares, and meat shares available as well.

Higher Ed

For those looking to expand their careers, Buffalo offerts many opportunities to go back to school and earn a degree. There's 19 colleges and universities within 50 miles of Buffalo. They range in sizes from big to small, some are universisites where one can get a graduate degree, and some cater to working professionals by offering night classes.
